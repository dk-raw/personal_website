Airbus A220-300 40

The A220 is one of the most efficient aircraft introduced by Airbus as the world’s most advanced and fuel-efficient single-aisle aircraft family. Perfect for 2 to 3 hour flights, the A220 will deliver unrivable comfort to all 140 passengers, fuel efficiency and Co2 emissions.

Airbus A319neo 25

The shortened-fuselage member of Airbus’ game-changing A320neo product line – the A319neo – seats 120-150 passengers in two classes or as many as 160 with a range of up to 3,750 nautical miles. Contributing to its operational efficiency is the choice of two new-generation engines, as well as the incorporation of large, fuel-saving wingtip devices called Sharklets.

Airbus A320neo 130

The A320neo is one of many upgrades introduced by Airbus to help maintain its A320 product line’s position as the world’s most advanced and fuel-efficient single-aisle aircraft family. The baseline A320neo jetliner has a choice of two new-generation engines from Pratt and Whitney and features large, fuel-saving wingtip devices known as Sharklets.

Airbus A350-900 90

The A350-900 is the cornerstone member of Airbus’ all-new A350 Family, which is shaping the future air travel. This jetliner accommodates between 300 and 350 passengers and its state-of-the-art comfort and amenities ensure that everyone on-board enjoys a pleasant flight. As with all A350 models, its fuselage is built with carbon-fibre reinforced plastic – which supports lower fuel burn and increased resistance to corrosion.

Airbus A350-1000 245

Measuring nearly 74 metres, the A350-1000 is the longest version of Airbus’ new family of wide-body jetliners. In a typical three-class configuration, featuring Athens Airways’ 18-inch-wide economy class seats for modern comfort, the A350-1000 seats between 350 and 410 passengers – who can expect a spacious and quiet cabin, wider seats and elegant ambient lighting that contributes to a beautiful interior while also minimising the effects of jetlag.

Bombardier CRJ-900 25

The Bombardier CRJ-900 Turbofan airliner is manufactured by Bombardier since 1997. It delivers unrivable comfort in all seats. It's comfort, flying and low infrastructure needs capabilities contribute to a better experience for both the passengers and the pilots.

Bombardier Dash 8 Q-400 32

The Dash 8-400 is the most productive turboprop on the market. Known as the “network builder” due to its short take-off and landing capabilities and efficient regional operations, it comes equipped with more seats, more legroom, more cargo and offers more flights per day.
