Airbus A320neo

The A320neo is one of many upgrades introduced by Airbus to help maintain its A320 product line’s position as the world’s most advanced and fuel-efficient single-aisle aircraft family. The baseline A320neo jetliner has a choice of two new-generation engines from Pratt and Whitney and features large, fuel-saving wingtip devices known as Sharklets.

Airbus A330-900

The extended-fuselage member of Airbus’ A330neo product line – the A330-900 – can accommodate between 260 and 300 seats in a typical three-class layout or up to 440 for high-density configurations. Incorporating the latest-generation Rolls-Royce Trent 7000 engines, along with aerodynamic improvements – including new composite Sharklet wingtip devices – as well as increased lift and reduced drag, the A330neo is a more efficient aircraft.

Airbus A350-1000

Measuring nearly 74 metres from nose-to-tail, the A350-1000 is the longest-fuselage version of Airbus’ all-new family of twin-aisle, wide-body jetliners. In a typical three-class configuration, featuring Athens Airways’ 18-inch-wide economy class seats for modern comfort, the A350-1000 seats between 350 and 410 passengers – who can expect a spacious and quiet cabin, wider seats and elegant ambient lighting that contributes to a beautiful interior while also minimising the effects of jetlag. The aircraft entered into service in February 2018.

Boeing 777-300er

The Boeing 777's unique combination of superior range, outstanding fuel efficiency and passenger-preferred comfort has created long-range success for carriers around the world. And the 777-300ER now gives operators a perfect opportunity to extend that success. Recent upgrades further reduce costs and boost revenue, and the 777's flying experience is still number one with passengers. A more efficient fleet and more satisfied passengers. That's a better way to fly.

Bombardier Dash 8 Q-400

The Bombardier Dash 8-Q400 Medium Turbo-prop is manufactured by Bombardier since 1997. The cabin measures 61.7 feet long by 8.2 feet wide by 6.3 feet tall giving it a total cabin volume of 3,236.4 cubic feet making it comfortable for up to 19 passengers. The baggage compartment can hold up to 71 bags. The Bombardier Dash 8-Q400 has a maximum range of 2,012 miles and a maximum speed of 393 mph.
